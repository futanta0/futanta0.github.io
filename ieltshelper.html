Understanding the Assessment Criteria

🎯

Task Response

Fully address all parts of the question with well-supported and extended ideas.

🔗

Coherence & Cohesion

Logically organize your ideas with effective use of linking words and phrases.

📚

Lexical Resource

Use a wide, accurate, and natural range of vocabulary, including less common items.

✍️

Grammatical Range

Demonstrate a command of various simple and complex sentence structures with minimal errors.

💡 General Vocabulary

These words and phrases are essential for any essay, regardless of the topic. They will help you to structure your argument, express your opinion, and connect your ideas effectively.

Linking Words & Phrases

To Introduce an Idea

  • First and foremost, ...
  • To begin with, ...
  • It is widely believed that ...
  • A primary concern is ...
  • The advent of ...

To Add More Information

  • Furthermore, ...
  • In addition, ...
  • Moreover, ...
  • Additionally, ...
  • Not only ... but also ...

To Show Contrast

  • However, ...
  • In contrast, ...
  • On the other hand, ...
  • Nevertheless, ...
  • Conversely, ...

To Show Cause & Effect

  • Consequently, ...
  • As a result, ...
  • Therefore, ...
  • Thus, ...
  • This can lead to ...

To Give Examples

  • For instance, ...
  • For example, ...
  • To illustrate, ...
  • A case in point is ...
  • Such as ...

To Conclude an Essay

  • In conclusion, ...
  • To sum up, ...
  • In summary, ...
  • All things considered, ...
  • In light of the above, ...

General Band 9 Vocabulary

For Arguments

  • Proponents: Supporters of an idea.
  • Advocates: People who publicly support or recommend a policy.
  • Detractors: Critics of a policy or idea.
  • Contentious: Causing or likely to cause an argument.
  • Pragmatic: Dealing with things sensibly and realistically.
  • Unfettered: Not limited by rules or controls.
  • Tenet: A principle or belief, especially one of the main principles of a religion or philosophy.
  • Prevalent: Widespread in a particular area or at a particular time.
  • Nuanced: Characterized by subtle shades of meaning or expression.
  • Empirical: Based on, concerned with, or verifiable by observation or experience rather than theory or pure logic.

For Impact & Change

  • Profound: Very great or intense.
  • Unprecedented: Never done or known before.
  • Inexorable: Impossible to stop or prevent.
  • Catalyst: A person or thing that precipitates an event.
  • Mitigate: To make something less severe or painful.
  • Ramifications: A complex or unwelcome consequence of an action or event.
  • Transformative: Causing a marked change in someone or something.
  • Erosion: The process of gradually destroying something.
  • Synergy: The interaction or cooperation of two or more organizations, substances, or other agents to produce a combined effect greater than the sum of their separate effects.
  • Epochal: Relating to or characteristic of an epoch or era; highly significant or momentous.

For Society

  • Socioeconomic: Relating to or concerned with the interaction of social and economic factors.
  • Disparity: A great difference.
  • Plight: A dangerous, difficult, or otherwise unfortunate situation.
  • In a concerted effort: In a coordinated and united way.
  • The populace: The people residing in a country.
  • Equity: The quality of being fair and impartial.
  • Autonomy: The right or condition of self-government.
  • Cohesion: The action or fact of forming a united whole.
  • Interconnectedness: The state of being connected with each other.
  • Infrastructure: The basic physical and organizational structures needed for the operation of a society.
