General Tips and Collocations

This section provides overarching guidance for achieving band 9 in IELTS Writing Task 2. Success hinges on having a clear opinion, developing ideas logically, using a wide range of academic vocabulary and collocations, and structuring your essay effectively. Always analyse the question carefully, decide what type of essay it is, and plan your thesis statement and topic sentences before writing.

Writing Tips

  • Analyse the task: identify whether you need to give an opinion, discuss both views, solve a problem, or evaluate pros and cons.
  • Plan before you write: take a few minutes to brainstorm ideas and organise them into paragraphs. Quality matters more than quantity.
  • Use cohesive devices: link your sentences and paragraphs logically using connectors like furthermore, however, for example, and therefore.
  • Balance your position: present both sides where appropriate but make sure your own stance is clear.
  • Check your work: leave time to proof‑read for grammar errors, inappropriate word choice or repetition.

Useful Collocations and Phrases

Familiarity with common word pairings will make your writing sound more natural. Here are some collocations that can be used across almost any topic:

  • Play a pivotal role: to have a very important influence.
  • Address the root cause: tackle the fundamental reason for a problem.
  • Promote sustainable development: encourage growth that does not damage the environment.
  • Strive for equality: work hard towards fairness or equal rights.
  • Implement effective measures: put in place actions that work well.
  • Alleviate the burden: make a difficulty or problem easier to bear.
  • Foster innovation: encourage creativity and new ideas.
  • Maintain social cohesion: keep society united and stable.
  • Curb the negative impact: reduce harmful consequences.
  • Pave the way for: create the conditions for something to happen.

Science & Research

Questions in this area may involve funding of research, ethical issues and the importance of scientific literacy.

Question Bank

Opinion Essays

  • Government should invest more money in scientific research. Do you agree?
  • Space exploration is a waste of resources. Do you agree or disagree?
  • Animal testing should be banned. Do you agree?
  • Scientific discoveries should be shared freely. Do you agree?
  • Science is more important than the arts. Do you agree?

Discussion Essays

  • Some argue that the government should fund scientific research; others say private companies should. Discuss both sides.
  • Some people think that we rely too much on science; others think we do not rely enough. Discuss.

Advantages/Disadvantages Essays

  • Artificial intelligence. Discuss pros and cons.
  • Genetic engineering in agriculture. Discuss benefits and drawbacks.

Band 9 Sample Essay 1 – Opinion

Question: Government should invest more money in scientific research. Do you agree?

Scientific research underpins progress in health, technology and the environment. Government funding ensures that projects serving the public interest, not just commercial gain, are pursued.

I therefore agree that public investment in science is essential. Private companies may focus on profit, whereas state funding can support fundamental research and ensure that discoveries are shared for the benefit of all.

Band 9 Sample Essay 2 – Problem & Solution

Question: Lack of funding for scientific research. What problems does this cause and what solutions exist?

Insufficient funding slows down innovation and can cause scientists to move abroad. Promising projects may be abandoned, delaying breakthroughs in medicine and technology.

Governments can increase grants and offer tax incentives for private investment. Public‑private partnerships can pool resources. Encouraging interest in STEM fields will also expand the research workforce. These measures can ensure science continues to advance.

Band 9 Sample Essay 1 – Opinion

Question: Tourism brings more benefits than drawbacks. Do you agree?

Tourism generates income and jobs and promotes cross‑cultural understanding. Many countries rely on tourism for economic growth.

Nevertheless, mass tourism can damage environments and dilute culture. I believe that while tourism has many advantages, it should be managed sustainably to minimise harm.

Band 9 Sample Essay 2 – Problem & Solution

Question: Overtourism in popular destinations is causing problems. What are they and how can they be solved?

Overtourism leads to overcrowding, environmental degradation and rising prices for locals. Heritage sites may be damaged by excessive foot traffic.

Governments can limit visitor numbers and spread tourists over lesser‑known attractions. Investing in infrastructure and encouraging off‑season travel can also alleviate pressure. Educating tourists about respectful behaviour will further reduce negative impacts.

Band 9 Sample Essay 1 – Opinion

Question: Vegetarian diets are healthier than diets including meat. Do you agree?

Vegetarians argue that plant‑based diets lower the risk of heart disease and reduce environmental impact. They claim meat is unnecessary for nutrition.

However, meat provides essential nutrients like iron and vitamin B12. A balanced diet can include moderate meat consumption. In my view, the healthiest diet is varied and includes plenty of plant foods, whether or not meat is consumed.

Band 9 Sample Essay 2 – Problem & Solution

Question: Food waste is increasing in many countries. What are the causes and what can be done?

Consumers buy more than they need and throw away edible food. Supermarkets discard imperfect produce due to aesthetics. This waste squanders resources and contributes to greenhouse gas emissions.

Governments can run campaigns to change consumer habits and implement legislation encouraging supermarkets to donate unsold food. Individuals can plan meals and store food properly. By valuing food, we reduce waste and protect the planet.

Band 9 Sample Essay 1 – Opinion

Question: Capital punishment should be abolished. Do you agree?

Opponents of the death penalty argue that it is irreversible and that miscarriages of justice can lead to innocent people being executed. They also claim that capital punishment does not deter crime more effectively than life imprisonment.

I agree that the death penalty should be abolished. Modern justice systems should aim to rehabilitate rather than kill. Life sentences protect society without violating human rights, and mistakes can be corrected if new evidence emerges.

Band 9 Sample Essay 2 – Problem & Solution

Question: Cybercrime is increasing rapidly. What problems does this cause and how can it be reduced?

Cybercrime costs businesses and individuals billions and undermines trust in online services. It can lead to identity theft, financial loss and national security threats.

Governments should invest in cybersecurity and enact strict laws against hacking. Individuals must use strong passwords and update software. International cooperation is essential to track criminals across borders. Education about online safety can also reduce victims’ vulnerability.

Band 9 Sample Essay 1 – Opinion

Question: Some people think that teachers should be replaced by computers in the classroom, while others believe that human teachers are essential. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

Proponents of using computers in place of teachers argue that technology can deliver information more efficiently and consistently. Digital platforms offer interactive simulations and personalised feedback that can be accessed anytime. In remote or disadvantaged areas where qualified teachers are scarce, computer‑based learning might provide access to resources that would otherwise be unavailable.

They create a supportive classroom environment and provide mentorship that machines cannot replicate. Furthermore, over‑reliance on technology can widen the digital divide, disadvantaging students who lack access to devices or reliable internet. In my view, while computers are invaluable educational tools, they should complement rather than replace human teachers. A blended approach that combines the efficiency of technology with the empathy of skilled educators is the most effective way to foster comprehensive learning.

Band 9 Sample Essay 2 – Problem & Solution

Question: In many countries, there is a shortage of new teachers in primary and secondary schools. What are the causes and what solutions can you suggest?

There are several reasons why fewer graduates are entering the teaching profession. Finally, the profession may suffer from a lack of status, discouraging talented individuals from considering it as a viable option.

To tackle this problem, governments could increase funding to raise teachers’ pay and improve working conditions. Providing opportunities for professional development and clear career progression would also make the job more appealing. Additionally, public awareness campaigns that highlight the importance and rewards of teaching could elevate its status. By implementing these measures, more capable graduates might be encouraged to pursue teaching, helping to ease the shortage.

Question: Technology will eventually replace most human jobs. Do you agree or disagree?

Some commentators predict that automation and artificial intelligence will render the majority of human workers obsolete. Manufacturers of apps and games should also incorporate prompts for breaks and educational content. By adopting these strategies, we can ensure that digital technology enriches rather than harms children’s lives.

Fillers and Discourse Markers

Using fillers and discourse markers can give you time to think and make your speech flow more naturally. Use them sparingly:

  • Well, …
  • You know, …
  • Actually, …
  • I mean, …
  • Let me think…
  • That’s an interesting question.
  • To be honest, …
  • On the one hand … on the other hand…
  • In my view, …
